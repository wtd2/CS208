\paragraph{Ex 3}~{\textbf{(b).}}
\par Suppose $n=2$ and the associated ratings of $\mathcal{A}$'s TV shows are $1,3$ and those of $\mathcal{B}$'s TV shows are $2,4$.
\par One possible situation is $S=(1,3)$ and $T=(2,4)$ (or equivalently $S=(3,1)$ and $T=(4,2)$), in which case $\mathcal{A}$ can change $S$ to $(3,1)$ to win more slots.
\par Another possible situation is $S=(1,3)$ and $T=(4,2)$ (or equivalently $S=(3,1)$ and $T=(2,4)$), in which case $\mathcal{B}$ can change $T$ to $(2,4)$ to win more slots.
\par As mentioned above, there is no stable pair of schedules in the given associated ratings.
